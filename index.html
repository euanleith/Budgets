Comparing the housing budgets of Irish political parties

New social housing

The most significant spending item is new social housing, with both the highest expenditure and difference in expenditure between parties. Again, FFG is spending the least at €1.8b, followed by SF, Labour and SocDems proposing increases of around €1-1.5b, and PBP proposing a greater increase of around €3.6b.

Summary of other items

There appears to be little difference between parties on other spending items, but this is partly because of the high proportion of spending on social housing. You can click on 'New social housing' in the legend to hide it, and make the other differences more apparent.

Items can also be double-clicked on to focus on them.

  • Cost rental / affordable purchase: SF +€200m, Labour +€100m.
  • Subsidies for existing private housing: PBP +€100m towards HAP programme in the short term, with plans to reduce funding over time. This plan is common among the opposition parties, and will be detailed in a later section.
  • Disadvantaged groups: Most opposition parties +€100m (increases to traveller accommodation, disability and elderly grants, and homeless services), and SF +€230m (greater spending on homeless services).
  • Defects: Differs a decent amount party to party from €70-190m, but PBP spending significantly more at €510m.
  • State construction company: PBP €500m. This is also being considered by Labour, but isn't currently costed.

The vast majority of expenditure from all parties is towards existing schemes. However, in the next few sections we will focus on the changes that have been proposed as they provide some interesting insights.

HAP/RAS

The opposition parties are all proposing to remove or significantly reduce expenditure towards the HAP and RAS schemes in the long run, while the incumbent parties have maintained their confidence in the schemes.
